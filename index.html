That Our Readers Love

  • Captains of the Sands

    "Capitães da Areia" is a novel depicting the lives of homeless children and teenagers in the streets of Salvador, Bahia. These youths survive through theft and solidarity, confronting challenges and societal neglect while holding onto hopes for a brighter future. The book delves into themes of poverty, resilience, and social injustice in early 20th-century Brazil.

    By Jorge Amado

  • Darwin sem frescura

    In a journey through eras, continents, and extinctions, two Brazilian nerds invite us to dive into the theory of Evolution with the most modern and varied scientific discoveries. After all, is there a missing link? Where did our species come from? Why do Irish people have more lactose tolerance than Chinese? How does Evolution explain the existence of homosexuality?

    By Pirula and Reinaldo José Lopes

  • Peter Pan

    "Peter Pan" is a timeless adventure tale about Peter Pan, a boy who never grows up, and the Darling children in the magical world ofNeverland. They confront Captain Hook and his crew, meeting whimsical characters like Tinker Bell and the Lost Boys. The story explores themes of childhood innocence and the power of imagination.

    By J.M. Barrie

  • Pride and Prejudice

    "Pride and Prejudice" is a timeless tale set in 19th-century England, following the spirited Elizabeth Bennet as she navigates societal expectations and matters of the heart. Amidst familial pressures and societal norms, Elizabeth encounters the aloof Mr. Darcy, leading to a journey of self-discovery and romance. Jane Austen's classic novel explores themes of love, marriage, and social class, with wit and charm, against the backdrop of Regency-era England.

    By Jane Austen
